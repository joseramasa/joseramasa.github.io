José Ramón Martínez Saavedra

VP of Innovation · Quside Technologies

Barcelona, Spain

I am a physicist and technologist leading research and innovation at Quside Technologies, where I serve as VP of Innovation and Lead Scientist. I work on taking quantum physics from the lab to real-world products — designing and building quantum random number generators, integrating them with post-quantum cryptography stacks, and pushing their use into high-performance computing and cybersecurity applications.

On a day-to-day basis, I lead Quside’s R&D strategy, manage our international patent portfolio (30+ families; co-inventor in 8 across US, EP, CN, JP), and coordinate competitive public funding. I also bridge the gap between our photonics hardware and the software and systems that make it useful at scale.

I hold a Ph.D. in Photonics from Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya / ICFO (2018, Cum Laude), supervised by Prof. F. Javier García de Abajo, and an MBA from Valar Institute (2025). My academic roots are in Physics at Universidad Complutense de Madrid.

I teach Software Architecture for Quantum Computers at UPC’s Postgraduate Course in Quantum Engineering, and supervise doctoral and master’s theses at UPC and ICFO.

Beyond physics, I am pursuing a Law degree at Universitat Oberta de Catalunya — a long-standing interest in the intersection of technology, intellectual property, and regulation. I have also contributed chapters to three popular science books.
