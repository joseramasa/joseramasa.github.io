I am a physicist and technologist leading research and innovation at [Quside Technologies](https://quside.com), where I serve as VP of Innovation and Lead Scientist. I work on taking quantum physics from the lab to real-world products — designing and building **quantum random number generators**, integrating them with **post-quantum cryptography** stacks, and pushing their use into **high-performance computing** and **cybersecurity** applications.

On a day-to-day basis, I lead Quside's R&D strategy, manage our **international patent portfolio** (30+ families; co-inventor in 8 across US, EP, CN, JP), and coordinate competitive public funding. I also bridge the gap between our photonics hardware and the software and systems that make it useful at scale.

I hold a **Ph.D. in Photonics** from Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya / ICFO (2018, *Cum Laude*), supervised by Prof. F. Javier García de Abajo, and an **MBA** from Valar Institute (2025). My academic roots are in Physics at Universidad Complutense de Madrid.

I teach *Software Architecture for Quantum Computers* at UPC's [Postgraduate Course in Quantum Engineering](https://upcschool.upc.edu/ing/estudis/formacio/curs/304400/postgrau-enginyeria-quantica/), and supervise doctoral and master's theses at UPC and ICFO.

Beyond physics, I am pursuing a **Law degree** at Universitat Oberta de Catalunya — a long-standing interest in the intersection of technology, intellectual property, and regulation. I am also a science communicator, having contributed chapters to three popular science books: [*Ciencia, y además lo entiendo*](https://divulgacioncientificadecientificos.blogspot.com/p/libro-book.html), [*Ciencia, y yo quiero ser científico*](https://cienciayyoquierosercientifico.blogspot.com/), and [*Ciencia y otras easy pieces*](https://cienciayotraseasypieces.blogspot.com/) — all freely available online.
